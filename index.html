about cubiko
Cubiko is a Practice Intelligence Platform. We make practice life easier with better reporting so that you know how you can improve your medical practice's business performance. Cubiko displays data in easy-to-understand dashboards alongside metrics and key performance indicators (KPIs). Use Cubiko's clinic optimisation metrics to increase billings and deliver greater patient care.

Cubiko solves large problems for a practice and their team:

- The stress of a practice owner trying to keep their doors open to continue great patient care.
- Feelings of being overwhelmed as a practice manager or owner due to lack of visibility over practice performance.
- As a nurse or doctor, confusion as to what patients are eligible for item number under a complex Medicare Benefits Schedule (MBS).



I was the whole design team at cubiko (now we all are)

I joined cubiko as a product designer a year ago as the first internal product designer. I support design across every aspect of our business and am responsible for leading UX and UI across key parts of the application side of the products.

I've grown tremendously in the last year, some key achievements of which I have listed below:

- Implemented a design process and mentored my peers into design thinking. This has helped our team establish more structure to how we conduct our work and allow other teams to gain visibility across our upcoming sprints.

- Improved usability across the platform. Not much usability tests were conducted by the external consultancy before dev handoff. Since we established design thinking across all teams , we have been actively working towards conducting UX research and usability testing on all projects.

- Establishing a design kit. This has helped to maintain consistency in the look and feel across different parts of the platform.



process

Our process at cubiko is based on the Double Diamond Theory and Lean UX process. We aim to incorporate the key phases of Discovery, Definition, Ideation and Implementation in all of our projects.



problem definition

Before cubiko hired a product designer, the product had been implemented and maintained by different deigners from an external consultancy. The systems were created without much usability testing and had little consideration for the further development of the product. I conducted research interviews with our primary users (practice managers) to uncover any pain points that they were experiencing with the product. ‍ My research encompassed: Understanding the user goals and needs Uncovering pain points with the existing user journey Determining the success of the tasks measured



empathise

After collecting the recordings from the user interviews, I conducted affinity mapping with the product Team to synthesise the pains identified. We grouped these problems under common themes and features in the platform.



I developed feedback pipelines for customer success teams to streamline previous and upcoming feature requests. Feedback then be categorised to go in support pipelines or aha! where we rate the features with product managers. Similar to severity framework, systems built in aha! does assist us to priorotise features. The framework identify the severity score of a problem based on the three variables - Task criticality x impact x frequency = severity

to measure we setup public voting for problems for products users to participate then follow up with surveys. Upon scoring the problems, cards then be created to insert appropriate places in the production roadmap.

in the big picture

I took the extra step of categorising these problems into broader Epics to provide the Product managers and engineers with visibility into the key features in the milestones that needed to be addressed from a usability standpoint. This not only helped to prioritise features in order of need but also helped to shape the product roadmap.
‍ Product, Design and Engineering teams came to the conclusion that the first Epic we would prioritise would be the product configuration and setup due to its severity score, UX and engineering effort required to rebuild it.


hammering the scope

Based on the user interviews conducted with multiple users, we found the following key issues:

- 70% of users felt that the process of configuring the product was tedious

- 19% were unable to complete initial configuration without live assistance

- 48% of users felt overwhelmed even before they start using the product



Wireframing the solution

Based on the above problems identified, I worked towards addressing these pains by coming up with potential solutions:

- Reducing the number of steps to minimise time to completion

- Create templates for users to configure their product express, easy or advanced

- redesigned the UI and the UX for intuitive setup process that is backed up by assistance bot

I quickly mocked up some basic wireframes to gather feedback from Product, Engineering and the users on the overall layout and structure of the configuration. This involved establishing a standardised visual hierarchy and layout for the future design systems and onboarding process.



Validation

I conducted usability testing sessions with our primary users to validate whether the new designs would solve their problems. I wrote a script including a scenario asking the user to configure their practice software for the first time with the desired option.

During the session, I observed how they interacted with the prototype and set up their product. The usability session revealed that majority of the user wanted to express set up their product. . User wanted to use the product first then could make changes as time past if they require further configuration.



Development

I updated the miro board that included the userflow and added low fidelity prototypes. Upon breaking down engineering efforts into small pieces, I assisted product managers to create cards and assigned some of the enginering cards to myself.

‍ I worked very closely with the Front End team to spec out any missing interactions that were not covered in the low fidelity mockups. I conducted a UX review of each front-end ticket that was implemented to ensure it was aligned with the designs before meanwhile i've done the styling via sass + bootstrap toolkit.



next steps

Since the implementation of the new rebuild of the configuration, we have seen a significant decrease in the number of support tickets lodged through the service desk. Additionally, I have received positive feedback from users about the simplified configuration of their product, saving them a large proportion of their time.

THE PATHFINDER - front-end developer

The Pathfinder application was a great project Liam Key was technical supervisor for, in which we had a small crew of three interaction design students, Bethany La Paglia, Lachlan Colliss and Yagmur Sarcin. We developed and designed a web based, data driven quiz that would direct students into the different types of career outcomes a graphic designer become. Elicia Simons also helped supervise with clear visualizations and designs of the project.

The project was a custom built template, made using jQuery, Javascript and the Greensock library for customised animations. Originally the project utilised Fullpage.js, however it was later discarded due to restrictions and limitations on customization. A bare bones responsive fullpage.js library was rebuilt from scratch, that utilised horizontal navigation slides.

The project turned out to be a great success, with the quiz being rolled out to thousands of students with positive feedback.

centralised design systems

communication is everything. i build design systems to reduce the amount of time spent in meetings, run workshops that are not interrupted by jargon and regulate consistency accross products and features.

design systems can bring new levels of consistency and scalability to both product and web design — additionally, it can enhance the way company stakeholders communicate both internally and externally. i design and build design systems that assist stakeholders with integrations, product workshops, delivery estimations, design and development processes. while these design systems create a seamless experience throughout ideation, i often pair them with dev environments built using a delicate balance of systems like sma and bem to convert comms into deliverables in no time.

i create variables that can manipulate a large scale of moving pieces in interfaces. these variables come in handy when creating new products and features, extablishing integrations, creating themes and white labelling products.
